Background

Legalizing and regulating marijuana for recreational purposes presents a bold new landscape for Canadian businesses and governments alike. What this new landscape might look like remains unclear, however, the Federal Task Force charged with informing upcoming legislation on the topic continues to study the issue and meet with stakeholders throughout Canada and abroad, including in those US states where recreational marijuana is already legal. But why is it illegal in most places and why did it take Canada so long to legalize it? First, it was perceived as highly addictive and historically been linked with Narcotics such as Cocaine and Heroin. Adiitionally, it was associated with bad lifestyles and with oppressed ethnic groups. Marijuana was associated with Mexican-Americans, and a ban on marijuana was seen as a way of discouraging Mexican-American subcultures from developing. (1)

Who is impacted by this topic?


The Government

The government has to spend less money enforcing laws against marijuana. They also need to spend more money on marijuana sales, and hiring marijuana farmers.


Blackmarket dealers

Blackmarket dealers will be one of the most affected by this topic. They will sell way less Marijuana due to the legal accessibility of the substance. Marijuana users will feel more comfortable purchasing it legally. Most illegal dealers will go out of business.


Current Farmers

Current Marijuana farmers will be able to grow their business exponentially, as they are no longer selling small amounts to underground black market dealers, but in a mass to government officials.


Retailers

Retailers such as Shoppers Drug Mart, Loblaws and the LCBO will want to start incorporating Marijuana in their sales. In doing this, they will gain much more customers and money


Narcotic manufacturing companies

Companies manufacturing heavy narcotics such as Cocaine, Heroin and morphine will most likely lose a lot of business after their customers have easy access to a better narcotic. People will stop buying hard drugs, and will start buying more marijuana.

What are the various perspectives on this topic?


Parents and Guardians

Parents are scared that their young children might be exposed to edibles, and may consume them out of curiosity, as they are often displayed in a colourful and fun manner. Parents are also worried about their teenagers and young adults. Currently, the consumption of marijuana is a very common thing among youth and young adults.


People in need of marijuana

Many people are in need of marijuana to treat medical issues, but are not qualified for a prescription. These people are for the legalization of marijuana to be able to treat their illnesses without the need of a prescription.

Political parties

Liberal

The current prime minister of Canada, Justin Trudeau was the leader in charge of the decisions regarding cannabis laws. Justin Trudeau is for the legalization of marijuana, and he is in charge of the Liberal Party of Canada.


Conservatives

Stephen Harper, in charge of the Conservatives states that him and his party are strongly against the legalization of cannabis.

Attempts to Solve Problem

NORML

Their objective is to eliminate all civil and criminal penalties for private marijuana use. This organization is for the Canadian government legalizing Marijuana, because they want the penalty for marijuana to go down. They do this by raising awareness on the cause, and planning rallies and protests, educating the public about their right, and everything they need to know about the benefits and issues of legalizing Marijuana. They also want to keep marijuana a safe hobby. They do this by advertising the risks of cannabis to children, and the steps a parents can do to protect their young children from THC exposure.

WHO (World Health Organization)
